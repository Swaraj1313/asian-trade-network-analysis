ASEAN Trade Sankey (2024)

This Sankey diagram presents a Trade Intensity Index (TII)-based mapping of intra- and inter-regional trade dynamics within Asia for 2024. It captures the relative strength of trade linkages by comparing bilateral trade flows against global benchmarks. The flows trace exports from ASEAN economies through key subregional groupings—Southeast Asia, East Asia, South Asia, and Oceania—towards final destination markets. The thickness of each link reflects trade intensity, highlighting structurally significant corridors rather than just large economies. The visualization reveals the role of Singapore, Malaysia, and Thailand as central redistribution hubs, while also illustrating the dominant pull of East Asia in shaping regional trade architecture. By structuring flows as Country → Subregion → Destination, it provides insight into regional value chains, supply chain clustering, and the depth of intra-Asian integration.
